Complete Trek Itinerary

1
Day 1

Dehradun → Sankri Base Camp

  • Morning pickup from Dehradun (6:00 AM)
  • Drive through Mussoorie, scenic mountain roads
  • Lunch break at Purola (200km covered)
  • Continue journey to Sankri village
  • Evening arrival (210km total, 8-10 hours)
  • Check-in to guesthouse at base camp
  • Trek orientation & gear distribution
  • Meet fellow trekkers, dinner
  • Night stay at Sankri (6,400 feet)
2
Day 2

Sankri → Juda Ka Talab (4km Trek)

  • Wake up at 7:00 AM, breakfast
  • Trek start through pine forest trails
  • Gradual ascent, snow patches begin
  • Reach Juda Ka Talab (9,100 feet)
  • Frozen lake in winter - stunning!
  • Set up camps near the lake
  • Hot lunch served
  • Evening around campfire, star gazing
  • Dinner & overnight camping
3
Day 3

Juda Ka Talab → Kedarkantha Base (4km)

  • Early wake up (6:00 AM), hot tea
  • Breakfast in snow surroundings
  • Trek through dense snow
  • Pass through oak & pine forests
  • Reach base camp (11,250 feet)
  • Set up camps at base
  • Acclimatization walk around base
  • Summit brief by trek leader
  • Early dinner (6:00 PM)
  • Sleep early for summit push
4
Day 4

Summit Day → Hargaon (6km+6km)

  • Wake up at 2:00 AM (dark summit push)
  • Hot tea & light breakfast
  • Summit push starts (3:00 AM)
  • Headlamps, snow walk through night
  • Reach summit by sunrise (6:30-7:00 AM)
  • Kedarkantha peak - 3,850m conquered!
  • 360° views: Swargarohini, Bandarpoonch, more
  • Group photos, celebration at summit
  • Descend to base camp (10:00 AM)
  • Packed lunch, continue to Hargaon
  • Camp at Hargaon village (8,900 feet)
5
Day 5

Hargaon → Sankri → Dehradun

  • Morning breakfast at camp
  • Final 4km descend to Sankri
  • Reach Sankri by noon
  • Lunch at local restaurant
  • Certificate distribution ceremony
  • Departure for Dehradun (2:00 PM)
  • Evening arrival Dehradun (10:00 PM)
  • Trek ends with lifetime memories!

Best Time to Visit Kedarkantha

Peak Season

Early Winter (Dec-Jan)

-5°C to 10°C

Heavy snowfall guaranteed! Thick snow blanket, frozen trails. Most beautiful but challenging. Summit push hardest!

Popular Activities:
Snow trekking, photography, summit conquest, camping
Good Season

Late Winter (Feb-March)

0°C to 15°C

Snow still present but melting. Easier trails, clear views. Good for first-time winter trekkers!

Popular Activities:
Comfortable trek, summit views, snow activities, beginners friendly
Off Season

Spring (April)

5°C to 20°C

Snow reduces, trails muddy. Rhododendrons bloom! Pleasant weather but less snow magic. Budget rates!

Popular Activities:
Easy trekking, flora photography, peaceful trails

Important Travel Tips

Gear Checklist

  • Trekking shoes (high-ankle, waterproof) mandatory
  • 3-4 thermal layers for extreme cold
  • Insulated jacket, down jacket essential
  • Woolen gloves, socks, balaclava must
  • Trekking poles for snow walking
  • Headlamp with extra batteries

Fitness & Health

  • Basic cardio fitness required—walk/jog daily
  • Start preparing 1 month before
  • Altitude sickness medicine (Diamox) recommended
  • Stay hydrated—drink 3-4 liters water daily
  • Avoid alcohol 48 hours before trek
  • Inform about any medical conditions

Safety Measures

  • Follow trek leader instructions strictly
  • Never trek alone or leave group
  • Carry personal first-aid kit
  • Mobile network available till Sankri only
  • Emergency evacuation plan available
  • Travel insurance with high-altitude cover mandatory

Pro Tips

  • Break-in trekking shoes before trek
  • Pack light—25-30L backpack sufficient
  • Carry energy bars, dry fruits
  • Camera batteries drain fast in cold—carry extras
  • Start hydrating 2 days before trek
  • Summit day: wear all layers, no cotton
